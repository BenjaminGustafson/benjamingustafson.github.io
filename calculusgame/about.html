A puzzle game that teaches calculus

The goal of this project is to develop a proof-of-concept game where the player solves puzzles, and learns calculus along the way. The game is not intended to replace an entire calculus course, rather to be a supplement for a traditional classroom course. The game focuses on building intuition for the core concept of Calc I, the derivative.

Students in a calculus course can develop a shallow understanding of the derivative if they focus too much on the algebraic mechanics of taking a derivative. The puzzles in the game aim to build intuition on the relationship between the graph of a function and its derivative.

The above shows an early puzzle from the game. The graph on the left represents some function, $f$, and the graph on the right is its derivative $f'$. The player sets the values of $f'$ by moving the circles on graph on the right. The puzzle is solved by setting the values of $f'$ so that $f$ hits the square targets.

We can add more targets (squares) and sliders (circles) so that the graph becomes smoother. The piecewise functions above approach $f(x) = x^2/2$ and $f'(x) = x$.

Students are often led to memorize derivative rules, but this leads to a lack of depth of understanding. A student should be able to give some explanation as to why each derivative rule holds. A calculus textbook might give proofs of the rules, but these proofs are above the mathematical reading level of the typical student.

Video games offer a potentially more compelling way to study calculus or other subjects. For many students, calculus is a class that they are required to take, not one that they elect to take out of interest for the subject. There is a greater burden on us, the people claiming that the student should take calculus, to provide motivation.
