John Venn

English Logician and Inventor of the Venn Diagram

John Venn in 1883

John Venn was born on 4 August 1834 in Kingston upon Hull, Yorkshire, England. In 1857, he obtained his degree in mathematics from Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge. In 1883 he was elected as member of the Royal Society. He died on April 4th, 1923

Venn's mathematical work mainly focused on logic, following the work done by George Boole and Augustus De Morgan. He published three books on logic, The Logic of Chance (1866), Symbolic Logic (1881), and The Principles of Empirical Logic (1889). Venn is best known for introducing diagrams of overlapping circles that represented sets and logical statements, which are now known as Venn diagrams.
