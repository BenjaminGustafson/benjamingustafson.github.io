The distance between two sets

Suppose you wanted to define the distance between two sets of points in a metric space. It would be nice if you could define it so that the distance between two sets is also a metric. Then for any metric space, we would get another metric space (whose elements are subsets of the original metric space) for free!

Let's call the two sets $A$ and $B$, and say they came from a metric space $(X,d)$.

An initial attempt might be to find the two points in $A$ and $B$ that are closest to each other, and say that the distance between the sets is equal to the distance between those points.
